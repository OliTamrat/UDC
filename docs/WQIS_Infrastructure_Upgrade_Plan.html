Section 2

Upgrade 1: DC Map GeoJSON & NHD Overhaul

Replace all hand-coded polygon approximations with authoritative government geospatial data.

Current Problem

The files dc-boundaries.ts and dc-waterways.ts contain manually plotted coordinate arrays. Ward polygons use ~16 points each (the actual legal boundaries have thousands). The “Anacostia watershed” is a single rough polygon, not the USGS HUC-10 delineation. Waterways trace approximate paths — missing the full tributary network including Sligo Creek, Indian Creek, Paint Branch, and Lower Beaverdam Creek, all of which are significant pollution contributors to the Anacostia.

Solution: Official Government Data Sources

LayerAuthoritative SourcePrecisionCost
Ward BoundariesDC OCTO — Ward from 2022 (opendata.dc.gov)Legal boundary precision, thousands of vertices per polygonFree
DC Zoning (NEW)DC Office of Zoning — ZoningPln (opendata.dc.gov)Parcel-level: R, C, M, W, CR, PDR zonesFree
Watershed BoundariesUSGS Watershed Boundary Dataset — HUC-10: 02070010 (apps.nationalmap.gov)USGS hydrologic unit delineationFree
DC Watersheds (local)DOEE Watershed Boundaries (opendata.dc.gov)DOEE sub-watershed delineation within DCFree
NHD FlowlinesUSGS National Hydrography Dataset — NHDFlowline (apps.nationalmap.gov)All streams, rivers, canals with flow direction, stream order, reach codesFree
NHD WaterbodiesUSGS NHD — NHDWaterbody (apps.nationalmap.gov)River polygon extents showing actual width of Anacostia and PotomacFree
Flood ZonesFEMA National Flood Hazard Layer (msc.fema.gov)FEMA-certified flood risk areas with zone designationsFree
Impervious SurfacesNLCD Impervious Surface 2021 (mrlc.gov)30m resolution raster / vector polygonsFree

NHD Waterways: What Gets Added

The National Hydrography Dataset for HUC 02070010 (Anacostia) will replace hand-traced waterways with the complete tributary network. Key additions beyond what currently exists:

NW
NW Branch + Sligo Creek

Full flowline from Layhill through Wheaton, Silver Spring, Hyattsville to confluence. Currently only partially traced.

NE
NE Branch + Paint Branch + Indian Creek

Complete upstream network through Fairland, Burtonsville, College Park. Connects MCPS schools to the watershed story.

LB
Lower Beaverdam Creek

Major tributary in PG County currently absent from map. Significant urban runoff contributor.

DR
Drainage Channels & Wetlands

NHD Areas layer shows wetland/marsh zones along the Anacostia corridor — Kenilworth Marsh, Kingman Island.

Upgrade 2: Real-Time Hourly Ingestion

Move from once-daily to hourly data ingestion to deliver on the “real-time” promise.

Current vs. Proposed Schedule

(real-time sensors)		Daily 06:00 UTCHourly (:00 every hour)USGS sensors update every 15–60 min. Hourly matches their refresh cadence perfectly.

AI API Cost Detail (Anthropic Claude Haiku 4.5)

The WQIS AI Research Assistant uses Claude Haiku 4.5 (claude-haiku-4-5-20251001) via the Anthropic API. The 3,848-token system prompt is sent with every API round-trip. Tool-use queries (fetching live station data) involve 2–3 round-trips, meaning the system prompt is billed multiple times per user question.

AI is the dominant cost at scale — it exceeds all other infrastructure combined.
At pilot scale (100 students), Haiku AI costs ~$985/year — within a $5,000 budget but not trivial. At district scale (500+ students), AI alone reaches $3,283/year, consuming most of the budget. At full DCPS+MCPS, Haiku costs $10,507/year — far exceeding any $5,000 allocation.

Cost control strategies (implement before pilot):
(1) Rate-limit students to 5–10 queries/day
(2) Cache frequent questions (e.g., “what is pH?”) to avoid API calls
(3) Restrict AI assistant to teacher/researcher accounts only; students use pre-built stories and scenarios instead
(4) Fund AI costs from EPA/NOAA/NSF grant technology line items, not from the $5,000 operating budget
(5) Implement prompt caching (Anthropic offers cached prompt discounts for repeated system prompts)

Recommendation: Stay on Haiku 4.5. Never use Sonnet or Opus for student-facing queries — the 3–5× cost multiplier is unjustifiable at scale. For research-only use ($47/yr), any model is affordable. For K-12 pilot, budget $1,000–1,200/yr for AI and implement rate limiting from day one.
